  这个展览不是单纯的“女性摄影师集合”，而是在重新组织日本摄影史的入口：谁被记住、谁被公共机构持续展示、谁此前只是以零散方式出现。

  *I’m So Happy You Are Here* is on view at Hikarie Hall in Tokyo from July 4 to August 26, 2026. Aperture frames the exhibition as a counterpoint and challenge to established photographic history, focusing on intergenerational Japanese women photographers from the 1950s to the present. ([Aperture][1])

  The signal is not simply representation. It is the institutional reframing of Japanese photography through women’s practices.

* **Why it matters:**
  这和你正在做的 Women’s Image / The Moon Index 很贴近：不是“发现几个被忽视的女性”，而是重新搭建摄影史的索引方式。

## Story 02

* **Category:** Art & Photography

* **Chinese Headline:**
  阿尔勒摄影节的核心焦虑：AI、后殖民与摄影的“真实”问题

* **English Headline:**
  At Arles, photography’s anxiety about truth becomes impossible to ignore

* **Chinese Summary:**
  Financial Times 对 2026 年 Rencontres d’Arles 的报道指出，今年摄影节的多个展览都围绕“truth in photography”展开，议题包括 representation、殖民遗产与 AI 对图像真实性的影响。报道提到的项目包括 Magali Paulin 对法国殖民遗址的处理、Ghana 独立后摄影史展，以及 Omar Victor Diop 对历史叙事中种族排除的再进入。([フィナンシャル・タイムズ][2])

  阿尔勒官网也显示，2026 年项目仍然强调摄影书奖、摄影收藏、媒体库与参与式档案等机制，说明摄影节不仅是展览场，也是摄影史生产与保存的基础设施。([Rencontres Arles][3])

* **English Summary:**
  The 2026 Rencontres d’Arles is foregrounding photography’s relationship to truth, colonial memory, representation, and AI. The FT highlights exhibitions that examine colonial remains, Ghanaian post-independence photography, racial exclusion in historical narratives, and photography’s unstable claims to fidelity. ([フィナンシャル・タイムズ][2])

  The festival’s own site also emphasizes book awards, collections, media libraries, and participatory archives, showing how Arles functions as both an exhibition platform and a memory infrastructure. ([Rencontres Arles][3])

* **Why it matters:**
  对你来说，阿尔勒值得关注的不只是“有哪些展”，而是它如何定义下一阶段摄影 discourse：摄影是证词、档案、技术，还是一种已经不稳定的真实机制？
  Arles is useful as an early-warning system for where photography discourse is moving.

* **Editorial Signal:**
  **Truth crisis / festival as infrastructure / postcolonial archive**

  Tate 宣布将于 2026 年 7 月 15 日至 2027 年 1 月 17 日举办 Ana Mendieta 大型展，这是英国首个聚焦她的 major exhibition。Tate 介绍称，展览将呈现她的标志性作品，以及新修复影片、早期绘画和晚期雕塑作品。([タテ][6])

  Ana Mendieta 的重新进入大型机构叙事很重要，因为她的实践持续连接身体、土地、流亡、暴力、仪式与女性主义艺术史。

* **English Summary:**
  Tate Modern will present the UK’s first major exhibition dedicated to Ana Mendieta from July 15, 2026 to January 17, 2027. Tate states that the show includes iconic works alongside newly remastered films, early paintings, and late sculptural pieces. ([タテ][6])

  The institutional return of Mendieta matters because her work continues to connect body, land, exile, violence, ritual, and feminist art history.

* **Why it matters:**
  这不是单纯的回顾展，而是女性主义艺术史中一个复杂人物如何被重新机构化、重新观看。
  The question is not only how Mendieta is remembered, but what parts of her work institutions choose to make legible now.
